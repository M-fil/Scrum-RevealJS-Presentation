Scrum

Framework

In this Guide, it is said that Scrum is a FRAMEWORK for developing, delivering, and sustaining complex products.

To fully understand the meaning let’s discuss the definition of the word “framework”. A framework is a set of basic elements and rules on which the development process is built.

It means that Scrum as a framework provides a foundation on which developers build programs.

Scrum

Transparency
Inspection
Adaptation

Scrum is founded on empiricism.

Empiricism asserts that knowledge comes from experience and making decisions.

There are 3 crucial elements of Scrum and every empirical process. There are

  • transparency,
  • inspection,
  • and adaptation.

Transparency

Transparency is in charge of that all main characteristics of the process must be visible for the Scrum team. For example, transparency requires that all participants of Scrum should know what the result of the project will be.

Inspection

The second crucial aspect of Scrum is Inspection. This word speaks for itself. Scrum users should inspect what goes well and what goes wrong.

Adaptation

The last aspect of Scrum is Adaptation and it appears after Inspection. It means that the Scrum team firstly needs to inspect its progress and then if something goes wrong it should make changes and solve problems.

Crucial Elements of Scrum

  • Transparency
  • Inspection
  • Adaptation
Let’s summarize this part. Scrum relies on Transparency, Inspection, and Adaptation.

Super Team?

But who are the heroes, who are this super team that adheres to these 3 principles? Let’s introduce them.

The Product Owner

  • Manage the Product Backlog

The first personage of this amazing framework is a Product Owner.

You may think that he is a person who owns a product that is a customer. It makes sense. But it isn’t exactly so.

The Product Owner is the single person in the Scrum team who can manage the Product tasks or Product Backlog. No one else on the team can change tasks on their own.

The Development Team

  • Develops a product
  • Optimal size: 3 - 9 people
The next hero of the Scrum team is people who design a product. People who want to become, from my point of view, 99% of Rolling Scopes students.

It is a Development Team, programmers who design the project.

The optimal size of the Development Team varies from 3 to 9 people. It has been proven that with this number of members maximum efficiency is achieved.

Scrum Master

  • Promote and support Scrum as defined in the Scrum Guide

The last person in the Scrum team, the person who knows Scrum as the back of his hand, the guru of this framework, is a Scrum Master.

His work is to promote and support Scrum as it is written in the Scrum Guide. He tries to help everyone to change interactions to maximize the value created by the Scrum Team.

Scrum Team

  • The Scrum Master

Let’s calculate the parts of a Scrum Team together. There is a Product Owner, the Development Team, and Scrum Master.

But this little number of members in the Scrum Team allows participants to communicate more and discuss problems more detailed.

Scrum Events

According to the Scrum Guide, there are 4 events that were created to inspect progress and make changes in the shortest possible time.

The Sprint

1 - 4 weeks

All events held during the Sprint. Sprint is one more crucial term in the Scrum. It is often called the heart of Scrum.

It usually ranges from 1 to 4 weeks and in this period the Scrum Team creates the most useable and potentially releasable product Increment.

It also should be mentioned that there are no holidays or any weekends after the end of the Sprint. A new Sprint starts immediately after the conclusion of the previous Sprint.

Okay, we understand the meaning of the Sprint but what we need to do before starting it? What are we doing before starting any important task?

Usually, we promise ourselves that we will do it tomorrow.

Sprint Planning

But as the perfect variant, we should plan our work. The Sprint also starts with the planning. In the world of Scrum, it calls Sprint Planning.

In this event, the team discusses the crucial question: «What can we do in this Sprint to release the product?» During the Sprint Planning, the Scrum Team also crafts a Sprint Goal. Also on this event, the team selects items for the Sprint from the Product Backlog.

Daily Scrum

no more than 15 minutes!

Okay, we planned the Sprint. It is cool. But I also said that Scrum allows the team to communicate more. There is a special event for this purpose called Daily Scrum.

But communicate more it was said very loudly. Actually, Daily Scrum is strictly held for no more than 15 minutes. Scrum Master teaches the Development Team to keep the Daily Scrum within this time-box.

At this event, each member of the Development Team tells everyone what he did yesterday and what he will do today. Thus, Daily Scrum is just a plan for the upcoming day.

Sprint Review

The next Scrum event is a Sprint Review.

It relies on the Scrum concepts of Inspection and Adaptation. This event usually takes less than 4 hours and holds at the end of the Sprint. The main purpose of it is to inspect the Increment and adapt the Product Backlog if needed.

Sprint Retrospective

The last event which was created to fix Scrum Team fuckups is a Sprint Retrospective.

It always holds after the Sprint Review and it is an opportunity for the Scrum Team to inspect itself and create a plan for improvements. It takes always less than 3 hours. By the end of the Sprint Retrospective, the Scrum Team should identify improvements that it will implement in the next Sprint.

Sprint Events

  • Sprint Planning
  • Daily Scrum
  • Sprint Review
  • Sprint Retrospective
Let’s view all the events process held during the Sprint.
  • We always start with Sprint Planning where we select tasks from the product backlog for the Sprint.
  • Then we have Daily Scrum which is conducted every day and takes no more than 15 minutes.
  • At the end of the Sprint we inspect and adapt our work process. Sprint Review helps us in it.
  • Finally, we have Sprint Retrospective where the team identifies improvements which it can apply in the next Sprint.

The Sprint Repeats

This loop repeats every Sprint.
